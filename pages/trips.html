Clemson Engineers for Developing Countries has been involved in numerous trips to the developing world since it's inception. Below, a few of our recent trips are highlighted!

2013Spring

The Spring 2013 team worked on a variety of projects from implementing quality control programs at a local CMU block plant to taking data for design groups in the US. They also assisted a CEDC partner, Tradd Cotter with Mushroom Mountain in training local agronomists in mushroom production, and built the shade and housing structure for the mushroom growing startup program.


2012Fall

The Fall 2013 team focused extensively on completing the final pump installation, completing the construction on the Cange Municipal Water Project. Working with the Haitian construction crew, the team effectively cut installation time in half from the previous pump.


2012Spring

The Spring 2012 team was one of the largest groups CEDC has sent down, and also had one of the largest tasks for a short term team: to complete installation of the first pump and installation of the three-stage water treatment system. The fact that the team successfully accomplished both tasks in just one week is a testament to the extensive pre-planning and organization efforts of the team in the US and the industry advisor, David Vaughn of Fluor.


2011Fall

The Fall 2011 team began the Adopt-A-Village program village assessments by traveling to six villages and assessing existing water distribution facilities, water quality, and village water resource needs. The team focused on gathering data to be used by teams in the United States to design solutions to the needs they noted.
